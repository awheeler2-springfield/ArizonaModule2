Key Principle

Different sources may discuss the same medical topic, but they do different kinds of work. Identifying the source type helps writers judge credibility, understand audience and purpose, and choose evidence that fits a professional healthcare writing task.

Model Documentation Statement

Identifying the type of source matters because different sources do different kinds of work in healthcare writing. A scholarly article, guideline, organization publication, reference source, or news article may all provide useful information, but they differ in authority, audience, and purpose. Knowing the source type helps a writer judge credibility and decide whether the source is appropriate for the professional writing task.
